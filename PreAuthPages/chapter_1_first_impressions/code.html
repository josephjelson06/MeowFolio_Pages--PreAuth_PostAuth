First Impressions: The 6-Second Rule

Before a human ever reads your experience, they've already decided if they like you. Let's master the subconscious art of the resume scan.

Recruiters are Speed-Daters

Think of your resume as a billboard on a highway. Recruiters spend an average of six seconds looking at a resume before deciding "Yes" or "No".

They aren't looking for every detail yet; they are looking for visual anchors. If their eyes have to work too hard to find your name, your current role, or your skills, they've already moved on to the next candidate.

Source: Eye-tracking study by Ladders (2023)

Mochii's Pro Tip

"If you can't tell what you do from 3 feet away in 6 seconds, your layout is too busy! White space is your best friend, not your enemy."

The "F-Pattern" of reading resumes

Make Your Header Sing

Your header is the "Prime Real Estate". It should contain:

  • check_circle Visual Hierarchy: Your name should be the largest text on the page.
  • check_circle Target Job Title: Tell them who you are before they read the rest.
  • check_circle Clickable Links: LinkedIn and Portfolio are non-negotiable.
THE MOCHII CHALLENGE

The 6-Second Audit

Open your current resume, set a timer for 6 seconds, and look away. Now, look at it until the timer rings. Can you answer these 3 questions?

01.

What is your primary job title?

02.

How many years of experience?

03.

What's your top skill?
